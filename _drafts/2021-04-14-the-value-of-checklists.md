A [2018 Hidden Brain episode](https://www.npr.org/2018/08/27/642310810/you-2-0-check-yourself) discussed the value of checklists in different contexts. Today they provide a critical tool in complex working environments where the stakes are high. 

## Definition
They are especially useful in situations of great exhaustion or high stress and therefore used in fields where those conditions are often met (e.g. pilots, astronauts, doctors or construction crews).

## Implementation

Within the IT Security field, checklists can be applied for example in the following contexts:

+ Installation/Configuration of systems, appliances or software
+ Digital Forensics and Incident Response

### Communication
The success of a checklist relies on the practical implementation by the responsible employees.
Therefore all affected employees should get the chance to participate in the creation process.
This might involve direct involvement in the form of group discussions or one-on-one conversations as well as some form of written feedback.
If for whatever reason this not a feasible approach, it must be at the very least assured, that representatives of different employee groups (junior and senior staff, technical and organisational knowledge etc.) are involved in the creation and revision process.
It goes without saying, that a top-down bureaucratic approach is not recommended.

#### Getting feedback
Following this thought it becomes clear, that the creation of a checklist can not be the last step.
After the work on a checklist is completed, all affected employess must be informed and asked for their feedback.
This happens ideally again in an open fashion, using for example some kind of collaboration software.
The discussion should also be accessible for future employees, which is why email or other hidden communication channels should be avoided as a feedback tool.

#### Changing the narrative
One crucial point is the changing of the narrative when using a checklist.
The default way of conducting workflows for which a checklist exists must include the usage of those documents.
Should this not be the case, a justification is necessary and an investigation should be conducted regarding the circumstances.
This does not imply that every deviation from the norm has to be regarded automatically as a misconduct.
It could rather be that the checklist does not cover an edge case or a specific scenario.
But especially those situations provide an opportunity to enhance the checklist and must therefore be accordingly recognised.

### Audience
A checklist can be helpful for people with different skill levels.
Junior staff receives a guideline to follow a process, increasing their level of confidence and their ability to work according to organisational standards.
Experts benefit in that their high working velocity or overconfidence do not end up in missed findings or objectives.
Both requirements must be kept in mind when designing a checklist.

#### Dealing with Experts
However, it might be harder to convince an expert of using a checklist. This group might perceive them as demeaning or even an insult of their intelligence. One possible approach to overcome this obstacle is to actively involve those experts by asking them for help in curiating and designing a checklist. People are in general way more likely to participate in a process when asked for help instead of being comanded to follow. Another positive side effect is the [transfer of ownership](https://medium.com/@warrentanner/heres-how-you-get-employees-to-take-ownership-over-their-work-ebe1f7ebf508), that proved in the past especially helpful when dealing with expert level employees.
